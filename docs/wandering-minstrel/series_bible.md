# Wandering Minstrel - Series Bible

## Collection Overview

"Wandering Minstrel" is a ballad poetry collection that explores the art of narrative verse. Over 300 days, the series presents daily ballad poems that tell stories, evoke emotions, and honor the rich tradition of ballad literature.

## Genre and Style

- **Primary Genre**: Poetry
- **Subgenre**: Ballad
- **Style**: Narrative, storytelling, lyrical
- **Tone**: Varies by poem - dramatic, romantic, tragic, heroic, folkloric

## Poetic Forms

The collection draws from various ballad traditions:

### Traditional Ballads
- Folk ballads (passed through oral tradition)
- Literary ballads (written by known authors)
- Border ballads (Scottish-English border narratives)
- Broadside ballads (printed and sold as single sheets)

### Narrative Styles
- Story-driven poetry with clear narrative arcs
- Character-focused poems with dialogue
- Historical and legendary retellings
- Contemporary narrative verse

### Musical Elements
- Refrain and chorus structures
- Rhythmic patterns suitable for singing
- Repetition for emphasis and memorability
- Lyrical language with musical quality

## Themes

Ballads traditionally explore:
- Love and loss
- Heroism and adventure
- Tragedy and fate
- Folklore and legend
- Historical events
- Social commentary
- Nature and the supernatural

## Structure Guidelines

- Each poem should tell a story or present a narrative
- Use of traditional ballad stanza (quatrains with alternating rhyme)
- Incorporation of ballad techniques: repetition, refrain, dialogue
- Natural length determined by the story being told
- Clear narrative progression with beginning, middle, and end

## Audience

Readers interested in:
- Narrative poetry and storytelling
- Traditional ballad forms
- Folk literature and oral traditions
- Musical poetry
- Story-driven verse

## Generation Guidelines

- One ballad poem per day for 300 days
- Random selection from ballad forms and narrative styles
- Authentic to ballad conventions and traditions
- No artificial word count constraints
- Each poem stands alone as a complete narrative
- Series automatically completes after 300 poems

## Publication Metadata

Each poem includes:
- Poem number (1-300)
- Title reflecting the narrative or theme
- Publication date and time
- Poetry type/form used
- Full narrative content
